  5. fluency: is the text fluent and coherent, does it form a good narrative / a coherent story?

1. Data Coverage
--- The text contains all predicates from the data and does not miss any predicates shown in the data.

Example with higher rating: Trane, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin, is based in Ireland. It has 29 000 employees.

Example with lower rating: Trane, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin, is based in Ireland.
Why? The text does not mention Number of Employees predicate from the data.


2. Relevance
--- The text contains only known/relevant predicates, which are found in the data. The text does not contain any unknown/irrelevant/unrecognizable predicates.

Example with higher rating: Trane was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin. It has 29, 000 employees.
Why? The text contains only such predicates, which are found in the data.

Example with lower rating: Trane, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin, is based in Ireland. It is a famous company.
Why? The text contains predicate which mentions something like Notoriety, which is not found in the data, therefore, it is an irrelevant predicate.


3. Correctness
--- When describing information about relevant predicates (those, which are in both data and text), the text depicts them with correct/proper objects. Also, the text correctly introduces the Subject.

Example with higher rating: Trane was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin. It has 29 000 employees.
Why? The text describes predicates, which are present in the data, with correct objects. Subject is introduced properly.

Example with lower rating: Trane, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin, is based in Sweden. It has 29 000 employees.
Why? It incorrectly introduces object for the mentioned Location predicate.

Example with lower rating: Coup, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin, is based in Ireland. It has 29 000 employees.
Why? It incorrectly introduces the Subject.


4. Text Structure
--- The text is written in good English language, i.e. it is free from grammatical errors and well-structured.

Example with higher rating: Trane was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin. It has 29 000 employees.
Why positive? The text is grammatically correct and well-structured.

Example with lower rating: Trane, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin. It has 29 000 employees.
Why? The text is partly non-grammatical; the first sentence violates rules for clauses in English.


5. Fluency
--- The text sounds logically correct and forms a coherent whole. There are no parts of the text you would change to make it sound better. The text forms a nice narrative.

Example with higher rating: Trane, which was founded in January 1st 1913 in La Crosse, Wisconsin, has 29 000 employees.
Why? The sentences are well-connected and form a nice narrative.

Example with lower rating: Trane was founded in January 1st 1913. Trane was founded in La Crosse, Wisconsin. Trane was founded in Wisconsin and has 29 000 employees.
Why? The text has unnatural repetitions (repeating the subject several times and repeating information which has been already introduced). One would (i) replace repetitions of subject with the pronoun, (ii) remove repetitions about Wisconsin, (iii) connect sentences and form a single one.
